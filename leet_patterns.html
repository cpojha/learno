15 LeetCode Patterns

Essential problem-solving patterns to crack coding interviews.

Introduction

Cracking coding interviews requires a systematic approach. These 15 patterns cover the most common algorithms and problem-solving strategies you'll encounter.

Patterns

  • Sliding Window: Useful for problems involving subarrays or substrings.
  • Two Pointers: Great for sorted arrays or linked lists.
  • Fast & Slow Pointers: Common in cycle detection problems.
  • Merge Intervals: Helps solve interval overlap questions.
  • Cyclic Sort: Effective for problems involving missing numbers.
  • In-place Reversal: Frequently used in linked list problems.
  • Tree BFS/DFS: Used for traversing trees or graphs.
  • Two Heaps: Solves problems requiring medians or partitions.
  • Subsets: Ideal for problems requiring all combinations.
  • Modified Binary Search: Extends binary search for custom scenarios.
  • Top K Elements: Useful for priority or frequency problems.
  • Knapsack (DP): A staple for optimization problems.
  • Longest Common Substring: Common in string comparison problems.
  • Topological Sort: Essential for dependency-based problems.
  • Trie: Useful for prefix-based searches.

Conclusion

Mastering these patterns can significantly improve your problem-solving skills and prepare you for success in coding interviews.
